Thepla


Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/4 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 cup fresh fenugreek leaves (methi), finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, gram flour, chopped fenugreek leaves, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, asafoetida, and salt.
  2. Add yogurt and oil to the dry ingredients. Mix well to form a crumbly mixture.
  3. Gradually add water as needed and knead the mixture into a smooth dough. The dough should be soft and pliable.
  4. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
  5. Take one dough ball and roll it out into a thin, round disc using a rolling pin and some dry flour to prevent sticking.
  6. Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat. Place the rolled out thepla on the hot tawa.
  7. Cook the thepla on one side until small bubbles start to appear on the surface.
  8. Flip the thepla and cook on the other side, pressing lightly with a spatula, until golden brown spots appear.
  9. Brush the cooked side with oil or ghee, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown spots appear.
  10. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls, rolling out and cooking each thepla.
  11. Stack the cooked theplas on a plate and cover with a clean kitchen towel to keep them warm.
  12. Serve thepla hot with yogurt, pickle, or any chutney of your choice.
