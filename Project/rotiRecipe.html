Roti


Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt, a pinch (optional)
  • Ghee or oil, for cooking (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, add whole wheat flour and salt (if using). Mix well.
  2. Gradually add water to the flour, kneading as you go, until a soft, pliable dough forms. The dough should be smooth and not too sticky.
  3. Once the dough is formed, cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
  5. Take one dough ball and flatten it slightly with your hands.
  6. Dust the flattened dough ball with some dry flour and roll it out into a thin, round disc using a rolling pin.
  7. Heat a tawa or griddle over medium-high heat.
  8. Place the rolled out roti onto the hot tawa and cook for about 30 seconds to 1 minute, or until bubbles start to appear on the surface.
  9. Flip the roti and cook on the other side for another 30 seconds to 1 minute, or until light brown spots start to appear.
  10. If desired, you can brush the cooked side of the roti with ghee or oil for added flavor.
  11. Remove the roti from the tawa and place it on a plate. Cover with a clean kitchen towel to keep warm.
  12. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls, rolling out and cooking each roti.
  13. Serve the hot rotis with your favorite curry, dal, or any side dish of your choice.
