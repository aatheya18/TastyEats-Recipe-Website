Chole Masala Sabji


Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (chole), soaked overnight
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon chole masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked chickpeas and rinse them under cold water.
  2. In a pressure cooker, heat oil over medium heat.
  3. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  7. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, chole masala powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Add soaked chickpeas and water. Stir to combine.
  9. Close the pressure cooker with the lid and cook on medium heat for about 4-5 whistles or until the chickpeas are cooked through and soft.
  10. Once the pressure releases naturally, open the lid and check the consistency of the gravy. If it's too thick, add more water and simmer for a few minutes.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot with rice, roti, or naan.
