Raasgula


Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the milk to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Once the milk comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low and add lemon juice or vinegar gradually while stirring the milk continuously. This will curdle the milk and separate the whey.
  3. Once the milk has completely curdled and the whey is separated, turn off the heat.
  4. Strain the curdled milk through a cheesecloth or muslin cloth to separate the chenna (curdled milk solids) from the whey. Rinse the chenna under cold running water to remove the sourness from the lemon juice or vinegar.
  5. Gather the cloth and squeeze out the excess water from the chenna. Hang the cloth for about 30 minutes to drain any remaining water.
  6. After 30 minutes, transfer the chenna to a clean surface and knead it with the heels of your hands until it becomes smooth and soft. This will take about 8-10 minutes.
  7. Divide the kneaded chenna into equal-sized balls and roll each ball between your palms to make smooth, crack-free balls. Make sure there are no cracks on the surface of the balls, as they may break while cooking.
  8. In a wide pan, combine sugar, water, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using). Bring the syrup to a boil over medium heat.
  9. Once the syrup comes to a boil, gently drop the chenna balls into the syrup one by one.
  10. Cover the pan with a lid and let the rasgullas cook for about 15-20 minutes on medium heat. The rasgullas will double in size while cooking.
  11. After 15-20 minutes, turn off the heat and let the rasgullas cool down in the syrup.
  12. Once cooled, transfer the rasgullas along with the syrup to a serving bowl.
  13. Chill the rasgullas in the refrigerator for a few hours before serving.
  14. Serve the chilled rasgullas as a delicious dessert!
