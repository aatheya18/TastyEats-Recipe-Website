Veg Biryani


Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, potatoes, beans, peas, etc.), chopped
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt (curd)
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • Whole spices: 1 cinnamon stick, 2-3 green cardamoms, 4-5 cloves, 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or cooking oil
  • Fried onions, cashews, and raisins for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee or cooking oil in a large pot or pressure cooker over medium heat.
  2. Add the whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, bay leaf) and cumin seeds. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, biryani masala, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add chopped mixed vegetables and cook for 5-7 minutes until they are partially cooked.
  8. Add plain yogurt, mint leaves, and coriander leaves. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  9. Drain the soaked basmati rice and add it to the pot. Mix gently to coat the rice with the masala.
  10. Add 4 cups of water and adjust salt if needed. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  11. If using a pressure cooker, cover with the lid and cook on medium heat for 1 whistle. Then, reduce the heat to low and cook for another 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let the pressure release naturally.
  12. If using a pot, cover with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through and water is absorbed.
  13. Once done, fluff the rice gently with a fork. Garnish with fried onions, cashews, and raisins if desired.
  14. Serve hot with raita or salad.
